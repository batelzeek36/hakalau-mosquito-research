# Hakalau Mosquito Eradication Research

Decision-grade research compiled to answer: **what's the best mosquito-control unit, and how many of them, to near-eradicate mosquitoes on a 15-acre property in Hakalau, Big Island, Hawaii?**

Live site: see GitHub Pages once enabled — `docs/index.html`.

## TL;DR

- The negative reviews of modern Mosquito Magnet are largely truthful. American Biophysics Corp (the original maker) entered receivership in November 2006; Woodstream acquired the brand and ran the quality down. Skip the entire ecosystem.
- The right buy is **27 × Biogents BG-Mosquitaire CO2** ($299 each) + **30 × Biogents BG-GAT** ($40 each), based on peer-reviewed eradication evidence on the same species (Maldives 2022 study: 93–98% suppression on a 41-hectare tropical island with this exact trap pair).
- Layer with Bti larviciding + weekly source reduction + contractor-deployed In2Care stations in deep gulches.
- **Year-1 cost: ~$25,860. 5-year TCO: ~$62,000. Expected outcome: 90%+ suppression in 6 months, near-elimination in 12–18 months.**

## Sources

All four research docs cite primary sources inline (peer-reviewed papers, manufacturer data sheets, AMCA / EPA / CDC / USFWS publications, BBB profiles, long-term consumer reviews). The synthesis recommendations doc has a consolidated source list.

## Methodology

Four independent research streams were run in parallel against authoritative sources, then synthesized:

1. **Brand quality + reliability** — was the user's intuition that "old Mosquito Magnet was great, new ones break" actually correct? (Answer: yes, well-documented.)
2. **Commercial alternatives + deployment math** — what to buy instead, at what density, for what cost on 15 acres.
3. **Hakalau specifics** — local species mix, the avian-malaria / honeycreeper conservation context, the active Wolbachia IIT program, Hawaii pesticide regulation, local pest-control contractors.
4. **Integrated suppression** — what to layer beyond trapping (Bti, In2Care, source reduction, monitoring) for true near-eradication on wet tropical land.

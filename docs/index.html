⚠️ About manufacturer "1 acre" claims

Every consumer trap claims 1-acre coverage. Independent field data says claims are 3–5× exaggerated. The Maldives elimination study used 6 BG-Mosquitaire traps per hectare = 1 per 0.41 acre. That's the number for honest math.

🤔 Why so many traps? Isn't 27 wasteful?

Fair question. Honest answer: mosquito physics. A mosquito only "sees" a trap once she's in its CO2 plume — and a CO2 plume doesn't travel far before it dilutes below the detection threshold. Field studies put the realistic capture radius at 20–30 meters per trap, regardless of how much CO2 you push through it (more output gives marginally more reach, not 10× more).

That's why no commercial trap exists that "covers 15 acres." The physics doesn't allow it. The 93–98% suppression in the Maldives study came from density, not unit power — 6 traps per hectare = 1 trap per 0.41 acre. Drop below that density and suppression collapses (a 2024 Florida study showed In2Care suppression failing at 3/acre vs. holding at 10/acre).

So 27 traps for 15 acres is actually slightly less dense than the published evidence base for near-eradication — not wasteful at all. And the new architecture below makes it even leaner: only 21 are active CO2 traps; 6 are passive sticky-card traps (BG-GAT, no power, no CO2, just deploy and forget). The original plan called for 57 trap units total (27 CO2 + 30 GAT). The new plan is 27 total.

Power for 27 traps. Without 27 outlets, 27 cylinders, or 27 disasters.

Tiered power (AC near house, zonal solar hubs in the mid-zone, passive traps deep) makes the 15-acre system actually manageable.

The three-tier deployment

T1
8 TRAPS · NEAR THE HOUSE · 0–100 FT

AC power via outdoor outlets

The 8 traps closest to the residence/lanai run from existing outdoor GFCI outlets through Biogents' weatherproof 33-ft extension cable. No batteries, no panels, no failure modes. ~$5/trap/year in HECO electricity.

Cost: ~$25 per trap incremental.

T2
13 TRAPS · MID-ZONE · 3 SOLAR HUBS

Zonal solar hubs (not per-trap solar)

Each hub: 300W panel + 100Ah LiFePO4 + MPPT controller in NEMA 4X enclosure, feeding 4–5 nearby traps via 12 AWG direct-burial cable. Voltage drop on a 200-ft trunk to a 5W trap is 1.1% — well within margin. Three hubs cover all 13 mid-zone traps.

Hubs win over per-trap solar because they consolidate 24 small batteries → 4 big ones. Bigger batteries last longer, and one shaded enclosure outlives 24 trap-mounted boxes baking in the sun.

T3
6 TRAPS · DEEP BUSH · PASSIVE

BG-GAT — no power, no CO2

The 6 traps farthest from anything are passive sticky-card stations. They catch egg-laying females (the most epidemiologically important mosquitoes) without electricity or attractants. Sticky-card swap every 6–8 weeks. ~87% reduction in Aedes bites in field studies.

Cost: $40/trap. Maintenance: ~5 min per swap.

The CO2 plot twist: yeast + sugar, not cylinders

🍞 What the Maldives actually did

The Soneva Fushi resort (41-ha tropical island, ~500 BG-Mosquitaire CO2 traps) achieved 93–98% suppression with yeast + sugar + water + insulator in 5L bottles, refreshed weekly. Per station: ~700g sugar, ~20–40g yeast (or Biogents' BG-CO2 powder), ~3L water. ~$2/trap/week vs. ~$25/trap/week for cylinder refills. A whole-property batch fits on a kitchen counter.

Field studies confirm yeast-baited BG traps catch as many or more mosquitoes than industrial-CO2 traps (Saitoh et al. 2017, Smallegange et al. 2010). Slower CO2 release rate, better match to mosquito approach behavior.

🔥 Tropical install gotchas (don't skip these)

Battery enclosures need breather vents. Sealed boxes condense water inside and cook the BMS. NEMA 4X polycarbonate + hydrophobic membrane breather (Bisonprofab).

Conduit everything. Pack rats chew 1/2" PVC. Use 3/4"+ PVC or armored AWA cable. Spray peppermint oil on cable runs quarterly.

Lightning protection. 8-ft copper-clad ground rod + Midnite SPD-300 DC surge arrestor at every hub. Bury all interconnects — airborne wire is an antenna for cloud-to-cloud surges.

316 stainless hardware only. Galvanized fails inside 18 months in 150"+ rainfall. Tilt panels 19° (Hakalau's latitude) facing south. Quarterly vinegar wipe to kill lichen and fern spores germinating on panel frames.

Common assumption that Aedes aegypti is "eradicated from Hawaii" is wrong — it was eliminated by DDT in the 1940s–60s but has re-established in Big Island pockets. Wyeomyia mitchellii is essentially guaranteed on any Hakalau property with bromeliads or heliconia.

The conservation case 🌺

Hakalau Forest NWR sits at 2,500–6,500 ft on the eastern Mauna Kea slope, sheltering the densest endemic forest bird assemblage in Hawai'i: 'i'iwi, 'apapane, 'akiapola'au, 'akepa, Hawai'i 'amakihi.

Avian malaria — transmitted by Culex quinquefasciatus — is the primary driver of honeycreeper extinction. 'I'iwi mortality from a single infectious bite is ~93%.

🐦 Why this matters for the property

The malaria zone is climbing with warming. Year-round transmission below ~1,250 m (4,100 ft); historical boundary 1,500 m, now moving past 1,700 m. If your parcel is below 4,500 ft you're in the year-round Culex zone.

Every wild Culex you kill removes a possible bridge between an infected lowland 'amakihi (the partially-resistant reservoir) and a susceptible upslope 'i'iwi. The property is effectively a buffer zone for the NWR. Real, not greenwashing.

Wolbachia IIT — Big Island status

  • Maui (Haleakalā NP): active since Nov 2023, ~500K sterile males/week
  • Kaua'i (Alaka'i Plateau): active
  • Big Island: planning phase. April 2024 USFWS: 5-year goal to deploy at Hakalau Forest NWR or Hawai'i Volcanoes NP. Draft CCP-EA published March 2025.

Private suppression on the parcel is fully compatible with IIT. Sterile males die naturally within ~1 week regardless. Killing wild females and breeding sites only improves the math.

📋 Hawaii regulatory notes

General-use (no license): Bti dunks/bits, pyriproxyfen tablets, BG-Mosquitaire CO2, Mosquito Magnet, consumer premixed barrier sprays, Essentria IC3.

Restricted Use (need certified applicator): Most concentrate bifenthrin (Talstar P, Bifen IT 7.9%) — on Hawaii's 2023 RUP list. In2Care is EPA-restricted to licensed pest-control operators.

Don't: introduce mosquitofish (Gambusia) into ornamental ponds — they're devastating to native Hawaiian damselflies. Native 'opae and gobies are far better biological control.

06 The integrated protocol

Traps alone don't do it. Six layers, weighted by importance.

▾

The Maldives study's 93–98% suppression came from traps + larval source management, not traps alone. On 15 wet tropical acres the breeding pressure is much higher — you need the full layered protocol.

🚶
01

Source reduction

Free, weekly walk. Gutters, tarps, plant saucers, tires, A/C condensate, rain barrels (mesh + Dunk), bromeliad axils. The "1cm of standing water for 7 days" rule because Culex + Aedes complete larva-to-adult in 5–7 days at Hakalau temps.

🦠
02

Bti larviciding

The biological gold standard. Mosquito Dunks (~$1) in standing water; Mosquito Bits in bromeliads; AquaBac 200G broadcast across gulches at 2.5 lb/acre (one 40-lb bag = 15 acres). Harmless to fish, frogs, birds, bees, mammals.

🪤
03

BG-Mosquitaire CO2 traps

27 units, perimeter belt + interior grid. NE-edge windward placement so the CO2 plume drifts across the protected zone. 200/300 ft perimeter spacing, 250 ft interior.

🍄
04

In2Care via contractor

Autodissemination — kills breeding sites you can't reach. Female enters station, picks up pyriproxyfen + Beauveria bassiana, contaminates dozens of cryptic sites she'd visit, then dies. ~10 stations in deep gulch zones, ~$400–700/mo.

🚫
05

Adulticides — minimize

No permanent mister systems. Pyrethroid drift kills bees, butterflies, native Hylaeus pollinators, native birds. Backpack ULV foggers and barrier sprays only for events — evening, never on bloom, never within 30 ft of streams.

📊
06

Monitoring

Measure or you're guessing. 1–2 BG-Sentinel-2 monitoring traps + 10–20 ovitraps. 2-week baseline, then week-over-week reduction. Target: 50% by week 8, 80%+ by week 16. If the curve doesn't match, density is wrong somewhere.

🚫 What NOT to do

Don't install permanent chemical misters. Pyrethroid drift kills native pollinators and birds; ethical and ecological non-starter for Hakalau.

Don't introduce mosquitofish (Gambusia). They're devastating to native Hawaiian damselflies.

Don't buy Spartan Mosquito Eradicator. Conclusively ineffective in peer-reviewed testing (Aryaprema 2020); $3.6M class-action settlement.

Don't run UV bug zappers as control. AMCA: zappers worsen ecosystems by killing non-target moths and beetles, don't reduce mosquito feeding on humans.

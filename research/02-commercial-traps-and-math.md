# Commercial Mosquito Traps & Deployment Math — 15-Acre Hakalau Property

**Site:** ~15 acres, Hakalau, Big Island, HI. Year-round wet tropical pressure; primary species are *Aedes albopictus* (Asian tiger) and *Culex quinquefasciatus* (southern house) — both confirmed across Big Island lowland wet forest fragments ([Reiter et al. 2018, J. Med. Entomol.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6201829/)). *Culex quinquefasciatus* is the avian-malaria vector being targeted by the Cornell/USFWS landscape-suppression program nearby ([Cornell Wildlife Health Center](https://wildlife.cornell.edu/blog/landscape-level-mosquito-suppression-protect-hawaiis-rapidly-vanishing-avifauna)).

**Goal:** Near-eradication ("basically free of them"). Budget open. Off-grid–friendly power preferred.

---

## Headline conclusion (read this first)

The only field-validated path to **near-eradication** in a small tropical landmass is the **Biogents BG-Mosquitaire CO2 + BG-GAT combination at high density (~6 traps/ha) plus larval source management (LSM)**. This protocol achieved **93–98% suppression** on a 41 ha Maldivian island (Jahir et al. 2022, *Insects*, [PMC9503984](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9503984/)) and complete *Aedes aegypti*/*Culex quinquefasciatus* elimination on Puerco Island, Philippines within 5 months ([Knols et al. 2023, PMC10531793](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10531793/)). Both targeted the same species you have at Hakalau.

Propane CO2 traps (Mosquito Magnet, SkeeterVac) work but have no peer-reviewed elimination data; they are best for **suppression around occupied zones**, not whole-property eradication. Yeast-tube products (Spartan) are conclusively worthless ([Aryaprema et al. 2020](https://colinpurrington.com/2020/05/class-action-suit-over-spartan-mosquito-eradicators/), $3.6M class-action settlement).

## Coverage reality vs. marketing

Manufacturer "1 acre" (≈4,047 m² ≈ 43,560 ft²) claims are aspirational. Independent field data:

- **Biogents BG-Mosquitaire CO2 (no booster):** 360 yd² ≈ 300 m² advertised by Biogents; **~20 m downwind plume** in field measurements per the Maldives study. With CO2 booster: ~840 yd² ≈ 700 m² ([Biogents shop](https://us-shop.biogents.com/products/bg-mosquitaire-co2)). The 41 ha Maldives elimination used **6 traps/ha = 1 trap per 1,667 m² = 1 trap per 0.41 acre** — i.e. effective coverage of about **0.4 acre per CO2-boosted Biogents trap** when deployed in a grid.
- **Mosquito Magnet Executive:** rated 1 acre but every consumer review and pest-control trade source caveats this to "line of sight" / wind-dependent / **0.25–0.5 acre in practice** ([Today's Homeowner 2026](https://todayshomeowner.com/pest-control/reviews/mosquito-magnet-review/)).
- **DynaTrap / Flowtron UV zappers:** "1 acre" is fiction. UV-attraction radius is ~10–15 m; AMCA's [Best Practices document](https://www.naccho.org/uploads/downloadable-resources/amca-guidelines-final_pdf.pdf) does not endorse zappers as a control method.

**Working assumption for 15-acre deployment math:** **0.4 acre per BG-Mosquitaire CO2 trap** in a grid (matches Maldives elimination protocol).

## Deployment math: 15 acres = 60,703 m² = 6.07 ha = 653,400 ft²

Square footprint approximation: 808 ft × 808 ft, perimeter 3,232 ft. Real shape and prevailing wind direction (from NE per Hawaii trades) drive placement, not raw count. AMCA guidance: traps go **30–100 ft inside the property line, on the upwind side**, in **shaded sheltered spots** ([Biogents placement](https://us-shop.biogents.com/pages/mosquito-trap-system-placement), [Mosquitalk](https://mosquitalk.com/where-to-place-mosquito-traps-for-best-outdoor-protection)).

### Scenario A — Minimum viable (perimeter belt + key gathering areas)
Belt of traps 50 ft inside perimeter, 200 ft spacing on the windward (NE) side, 300 ft on leeward sides; plus 4 traps interior around house/lanai.

- Windward perimeter (NE side, ~808 ft): 808 / 200 ≈ **5 traps**
- Other three sides (~2,424 ft total): 2,424 / 300 ≈ **8 traps**
- Interior at house/gathering: **4 traps**
- **Total: 17 BG-Mosquitaire CO2 traps**
- Coverage outcome: strong suppression around house/lanai, moderate (~60–75%) interior reduction, mosquitoes still detectable far from belt.

### Scenario B — Recommended (perimeter + interior partial grid)
Same perimeter belt, plus interior grid at 250 ft spacing across cleared/used acreage (~10 of 15 acres).

- Perimeter: **17 traps** as above
- Interior grid over 10 used acres (~40,470 m²) at 1 trap per 1,667 m² (Maldives density on used zones): 40,470 / 1,667 ≈ **24 additional**
- **Total: ~25–28 BG-Mosquitaire CO2 traps + 30 BG-GAT (oviposition) traps**

This is the **only** scenario with meaningful eradication probability based on published evidence.

### Scenario C — Maximum (full-property Maldives protocol, 6 traps/ha)
Direct port of the Jahir 2022 protocol: 6 BG-Mosquitaire CO2/ha + 7.2 BG-GAT/ha + LSM.

- BG-Mosquitaire CO2: 6 × 6.07 ha = **37 traps**
- BG-GAT: 7.2 × 6.07 = **44 oviposition traps**
- This is the densest published-evidence protocol that produced **near-zero mosquito populations** in 12–18 months in tropical conditions.

## Comparison to professional barrier service

[Lawn Love 2026](https://lawnlove.com/blog/mosquito-control-cost/) and [Angi 2026](https://www.angi.com/articles/cost-mosquito-treatment.htm) data for >1-acre properties: **$250–$500 per spray treatment**, every 3 weeks during season. Hawaii has no off-season, so plan 17 treatments/yr.

- 15-acre property scaling factor (50–100% premium per acre over 1-acre baseline): figure $800–$1,500/treatment
- Annual cost: 17 × $1,200 = **~$20,400/yr** — and this is *barrier* (perimeter only), which professional companies do not effectively scale to 15 acres in dense tropical pressure.
- Big Island operators (VDCI, Able Pest Hawaii, Kilauea Pest Control) typically refuse to quote 15-acre wet-tropical residential because adulticide barriers don't hold against a continuous breeding source. [VDCI Hawaii](https://www.vdci.net/hawaii-mosquito-control-a-vector-disease-control-international-vdci-company/) does integrated programs but pricing is project-based.

The trap-based plan **cuts annual cost by ~40%** versus professional spraying *and* targets the breeding cycle (essential for elimination) rather than just adult intercept.

## Larval Source Management (LSM) — non-negotiable

The Maldives study's success was **traps + LSM**, not traps alone. LSM is free and labor-only:

1. Walk the property monthly and dump every standing-water source (tarps, buckets, plant saucers, bromeliad axils, clogged gutters, tire tracks).
2. Bti briquettes (*Bacillus thuringiensis israelensis* — Mosquito Dunks, ~$15 / 6-pack) in any standing water you can't drain (ponds, water troughs).
3. Stock any ornamental ponds with mosquitofish (*Gambusia*) or native gobies — vector-control standard.

LSM alone reduces baseline by 30–50%; LSM + Scenario B traps is what produced 93–98% suppression in Jahir 2022.

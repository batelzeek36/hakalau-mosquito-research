# Integrated Suppression Protocol — Beyond CO2 Traps

**Property:** 15 acres, Hakalau, Big Island (windward, wet tropical, ~120-150 in/yr rainfall)
**Goal:** near-eradication, not nuisance reduction
**Companion to:** CO2 trap research (BG-Mosquitaire, Mosquito Magnet)

CO2 traps kill flying females. They do **not** suppress 15 acres of wet tropical land alone. Breeding pressure on a Hakalau property — bromeliads, gulches, leaf axils, hapu'u boots, gutter litter — has to be hit at the larval stage and via autodissemination. This is AMCA Integrated Mosquito Management (IMM): source reduction + larvicides + autodissemination + targeted adulticides + monitoring, layered. ([AMCA BMP](https://www.naccho.org/uploads/downloadable-resources/amca-guidelines-final_pdf.pdf))

Hakalau context: the dominant pest is *Culex quinquefasciatus*, also the vector of avian malaria killing native honeycreepers (iiwi mortality ~93%). Suppressing on your property has conservation value beyond personal comfort. ([PLOS One](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0168880))

## 1. Bti — the gold-standard biological larvicide

**Active ingredient:** *Bacillus thuringiensis* subsp. *israelensis*. A naturally occurring soil bacterium whose crystalline endotoxin only activates in the alkaline, receptor-bearing midgut of mosquito, black fly, and chironomid (midge) larvae. ([EPA Bti page](https://www.epa.gov/mosquitocontrol/bti-mosquito-control), [CDC Bti factsheet](https://www.cdc.gov/mosquitoes/pdfs/bti-508.pdf))

**Non-target safety (load-bearing for Hakalau):**
- **Fish, frogs, turtles, mammals, birds, bees, and most aquatic invertebrates: harmless.** They lack the alkaline gut + receptor combination needed to activate the toxin. ([WA DOH](https://doh.wa.gov/community-and-environment/pests/mosquitoes/bti), [Maryland Dept Ag factsheet](https://mda.maryland.gov/plants-pests/documents/bti.factsheet.pdf))
- Will affect non-target Diptera (midges/blackflies) — relevant if you have a stream where coqui prey on midges, but the food-web impact is generally accepted as far smaller than what adulticides cause.

**SKUs to deploy on the property:**

| Product | Form | Persistence | Best use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mosquito Dunks (Summit) | donut briquette | ~30 days | rain barrels, ornamental ponds, gulch puddles, semi-permanent water |
| Mosquito Bits (Summit) | corn-cob granule | ~7-14 days, kills <24h | bromeliads, plant saucers, gutter pockets, rapid-knockdown blitz |
| VectoBac WDG / G (Valent BioSciences) | water-dispersible granule / corn cob granule | ~7-14 days | broadcast across gulches and wet acreage at 2.5–10 lb/acre ([VectoBac WDG](https://www.valentbiosciences.com/publichealth/products/vectobac/water-dispersible-granules/)) |
| AquaBac 200G (Becker Microbial) | granular | ~14 days | same role as VectoBac; 2.5–10 lb/acre, 10–20 lb/acre if water is murky/algal ([AquaBac 200G label](https://www.arbico-organics.com/product/aquabac-bti-mosquito-control-granular-bacillus-thuringiensis-israelensis-bti/free-shipping)) |

**Application across 15 acres:** map zones — house perimeter (gutters, tarps, A/C condensate), ornamentals (bromeliads, gingers, hapu'u), gulch courses, troughs / rain barrels. Treat containerized water with Dunks (one per 100 sq ft of surface). For broadcasted areas (wet swales, gulch puddles), use a chest-mount granular spreader with VectoBac G or AquaBac 200G at low label rate (2.5 lb/acre). 15 acres × 2.5 lb = ~38 lb/treatment; a 40 lb bag covers one pass.

**Retreatment in continuous rain:** **every 7-10 days during heavy rain stretches** (granules wash out faster than the 30-day label). Drier weeks: every 14-21 days. Bromeliad flush-and-bit weekly. ([UF/IFAS](https://edis.ifas.ufl.edu/publication/IN1315))

**Cost (2026 retail):** Mosquito Dunks ~$0.80-1.20 each in 20-packs; Bits ~$15/30oz; AquaBac 200G ~$170-220/40 lb bag.

**Hawaii availability:** Bti is EPA-registered and routinely used by HDOA / DLNR — it's the larvicide being aerially deployed on Maui and Kauaʻi for forest-bird conservation. Mosquito Dunks/Bits ship to Hawaii from any major retailer. No special homeowner permit needed. ([HPR coverage](https://www.hawaiipublicradio.org/local-news/2024-07-01/mosquito-larvicide-bti-maui-kauai), [DLNR](https://dlnr.hawaii.gov/dofaw/fw-announcements/kauai/mosquito-updates/))

## 2. Spinosad — secondary larvicide

Fermentation-derived from *Saccharopolyspora spinosa*. **Natular (Clarke)** is pure spinosad; some Mosquito Bits SKUs combine Bti + spinosad. Spinosad reaches *all* larval instars (Bti is best on early instars) and has longer persistence in some formulations. **Use when:** (a) larvae are mostly late 3rd/4th instar, (b) you need longer residual in a hard-to-revisit gulch site, or (c) you want to rotate actives. **Caveat:** higher non-target risk to aquatic invertebrates than Bti — avoid in streams with native damselflies. Bti is default; spinosad is the rotation/escalation tool.

## 3. IGRs — Insect Growth Regulators

**Methoprene (Altosid):** juvenile-hormone mimic; larvae develop normally to pupa, then die. ProVect briquets and Altosid pellets release for ~30 days. Better residual than Bti, but **not fish/frog-safe in some formulations** — read the label, and don't use in waters with native ʻoʻopu (gobies). ([Methoprene info](https://mosquitoreviews.com/insecticides/methoprene/))

**Pyriproxyfen (Sumilarv 0.5G, NyGuard):** newer juvenile-hormone analog; lethal at parts-per-trillion. **The active ingredient In2Care relies on for its kill chain.** ([CDC larvicides](https://www.cdc.gov/mosquitoes/mosquito-control/larvicides.html))

**When to combine vs. substitute Bti:** the AMCA / mosquito-district pattern is **Bti for routine, IGR for problem sites**: a gulch puddle that you can only reach monthly, or a permanent ornamental pond where 30-day persistence beats weekly Bti retreatment. Do not need both in the same water — pick one and rotate seasonally.

## 4. In2Care Mosquito Station — autodissemination (most important layer)

**Mechanism:** a black bucket holding water with an oviposition lure and a gauze dosed with **pyriproxyfen** (PPF) + **Beauveria bassiana** spores. A gravid female enters to lay, contacts the gauze, picks up PPF dust + fungal spores, *fails* to lay viable eggs in the station (PPF-dosed water kills any larvae), then flies out to her other breeding sites — bromeliads, leaf axils, gulch puddles — and **contaminates each with PPF**, killing larvae in containers you never see. Fungus kills her ~10-14 days later. One station reaches dozens of cryptic sites per contaminated female. ([In2Care](https://www.in2care.org/), [Snetselaar et al. 2014 JAMCA](https://in2care.org/marketing/files/In2Care_Mosquito_Station_Factsheet_Jan_2023.pdf))

**Field-trial efficacy:**

- **Manatee County, FL — Buckner et al. (JAMCA 2017 / 2021):** semifield + 6-month large-scale field study. Stations alone produced **60% / 57% / 57%** reduction in *Aedes aegypti* eggs / larvae / adults vs. routine integrated vector management control. ([Buckner 2017 BioOne](https://bioone.org/journals/Journal-of-the-American-Mosquito-Control-Association/volume-33/issue-3/17-6642R.1/Evaluating-the-Vector-Control-Potential-of-the-In2Care-Mosquito-Trap/10.2987/17-6642R.1.short))
- **Australia — *Aedes notoscriptus*:** 110 stations across 50,000 m² (≈12.4 acres, 1 station / 450 m²) → **43% fewer eggs at 6 weeks**, **50% reduction in adult eclosion**, fungal nettings showed **93.4% spore germination**, **4× higher mortality** in B. bassiana-exposed adults, PPF detected dispersed across the treatment area. ([Liu et al. 2023 PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10496431/))
- **Florida (low-density follow-up, 2024 JME):** at the recommended ~10 stations/acre density, suppression holds for *Aedes aegypti* and *Culex quinquefasciatus*. **At 3 stations/acre, suppression collapses.** ([JME 2024 low-density study](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39093689/), [Entomology Today summary](https://entomologytoday.org/2024/09/12/researchers-seek-right-density-bait-stations-suppress-mosquito-numbers/))

**Density requirement: 1 station per 400 m² ≈ 10/acre is non-negotiable.** This is the manufacturer spec and the empirical break-even point.

**Refill schedule:** new In2Mix sachet **every 4 weeks** in tropical conditions (Hakalau), every 6 weeks in cooler climates. Refilling drift to 8 weeks is the most common operator failure mode.

**Recommendation for the 15-acre Hakalau property:**

- **Wall-to-wall: ~150 stations** at 10/acre. **High-use only: 40 stations** for the main 4 outdoor-use acres.
- **Recommended middle path: 60 stations covering 6 acres of high-use perimeter** (residence, lanai, garden, gulch lip). The deep 9 acres of gulch keeps producing mosquitoes that partially refill, but autodissemination + CO2 traps should hold the high-use zone at 80%+ knockdown.
- **Refill cost/year:** 60 stations × 13 refills/yr × ~$5.50/sachet = **~$4,290/yr**. Stations are ~$12-15 each one-time. At 150 stations: ~$10,725/yr.
- **Expected kill rate:** Manatee field data shows **57-60% reduction** in adults at full density standalone. Combined with CO2 traps + Bti, target **80-90% suppression** in 8-12 weeks.

**Purchasability — 2026:** **professional-use-only by EPA label and contract.** Not sold direct to homeowners. Distribution: SiteOne, Target Specialty Products, PestWeb / Veseris, Pest Control Superstore. ([SiteOne](https://www.siteone.com/en/in2care-stn-in2care-mosquito-station-black-qgcy-sold-by-each/p/850214), [Target Specialty](https://www.target-specialty.com/in2care-mosquito-station-mcp-25-black), [Veseris](https://veseris.com/default/insects/traps/mosquito-traps/in2care-mosquito-station-black))

**Workaround:** contract a licensed Hawaii pest-control operator to deploy + service. Commercial pricing: ~$80/mo for 2-3 stations; for 60 stations expect $400-700/mo all-in.

## 5. Source reduction protocol (wet tropical, 15 acres)

The "1 cm of standing water for 7 days = mosquito factory" rule comes from *Culex* and *Aedes* both completing larva-to-adult in 5-7 days at Hakalau temperatures. The walk-through must be **weekly** in wet season, every 2 weeks in drier stretches.

**Weekly checklist:**
- **Gutters / downspouts** — flush, clear leaf litter, install gutter screens
- **Tarps & canopies** — stretch tight, never let sag; collapse and store when not actively in use
- **Bromeliads / leaf axils / hapu'u boots** — flush with hose weekly OR drop a pinch of Mosquito Bits into each tank ([UF/IFAS bromeliad protocol](https://edis.ifas.ufl.edu/publication/IN1315))
- **Plant saucers** — drain or fill with sand
- **Recycling bins / buckets / wheelbarrows** — flip upside down or drill drainage holes
- **Tires (incl. tire swings)** — drill or remove
- **Boats / kayaks** — flip, cover with tight tarp, or store under roof
- **Birdbaths / pet bowls** — refresh every 3 days
- **A/C condensate lines** — point to dry surface, not pooling
- **Tree holes** — fill with sand or expanding foam
- **Tree-fern (hapu'u) crowns** — Mosquito Bits, monthly
- **Gulches** — broadcast Bti granular after every heavy rain pulse
- **Ornamental ponds** — Bti dunk monthly; **do NOT introduce mosquitofish (Gambusia)** — they're already devastating to native Hawaiian damselflies and stream invertebrates ([USGS NAS](https://nas.er.usgs.gov/queries/factsheet.aspx?SpeciesID=846)). Native ʻopae and gobies are far superior biological control.
- **Swimming pools (algae phase)** — 5 ppm chlorine or treat with Bti weekly until restored
- **Rain barrels** — mandatory: 1mm stainless mesh on inlet + 1 Mosquito Dunk/month inside ([CDC Aedes control PDF](https://www.cdc.gov/mosquitoes/pdfs/mosquito-control-508.pdf))

## 6. Adulticides — minimize, don't broadcast

**Hakalau ethical context:** native pollinators (Hylaeus / yellow-faced bees, several federally endangered), native birds, wet ecosystem where pyrethroid drift into a stream is real. **Default posture: no broadcast adulticides.** Reserve for emergent need (event, disease scare) or spot-treatment of resting sites.

**Permanent mister systems (Mistking, MistAway):** **do not install.** Bifenthrin/permethrin from these is highly toxic to bees, butterflies, and aquatic invertebrates; drift into adjacent forest is unavoidable. ([AMCA Pollinator Protections](https://www.mosquito.org/pollinator-protections/), [MSU Extension](https://www.canr.msu.edu/news/potential-impact-of-mosquito-and-nuisance-insect-sprays-on-pollinators))

**Backpack ULV foggers:** **pre-event spot use only** — 90 min before guests, evening after pollinators stop, never within 20 ft of bloom or water.

**Barrier sprays (Talstar / Bifen IT 7.9% bifenthrin, Suspend SC):** 30-day residual on vegetation 4-6 ft up. **Highly toxic to bees on contact and via flower residue.** ([DIY Pest Control](https://diypestcontrol.com/talstar-pro-one-insecticide)) If used: spot only, evening, dry weather, never on flowering plants, never within 30 ft of streams. Pollinator-safer alternative: **Essentria IC3** (rosemary/peppermint/geraniol) — shorter residual, pricier.

**Personal protection (always-on):**
- Permethrin-treated outerwear (Sawyer kit) — clothes only, lasts ~6 weeks / 6 washes
- DEET 20-30%, picaridin 20%, or oil of lemon eucalyptus / PMD on skin

## 7. Monitoring — measure or you're guessing

- **BG-Sentinel-2 (1-2 units, monitoring mode):** runs continuously with BG-Lure + CO2; weekly count gives a population index. Studies show 64-87% reduction in *Aedes albopictus* in BG-Sentinel-treated zones. ([Englbrecht et al. 2015](https://eu.biogents.com/wp-content/uploads/Englbrecht-et-al-2015-Evaluation-of-BG-Sentinel-Trap-as-Management-Tool-to-Reduce-Aedes-albopictus-Nuisance-in-Cesena.pdf), [Biogents BG-S2](https://www.nhbs.com/biogents-bg-sentinel-2-mosquito-trap))
- **Ovitraps / oviposition cups (10-20 across property):** black plastic cups, water + hardboard paddle. Count eggs weekly. Cheap, sensitive, the standard for measuring autodissemination effect. ([CDC AGO trap](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4631065/))
- **CDC light trap:** older, less *Aedes*-selective; secondary tool.

**Workflow:** **2-week baseline before any intervention** → deploy → measure week-over-week reduction. Target: 50% reduction by week 8, 80%+ by week 16. If you don't see this curve, density of stations / Bti retreatment is wrong.

## 8. Phased protocol for the Hakalau 15-acre

**Weeks 1-2 — Knockdown / source-reduction blitz:**
- Walk every acre, eliminate or modify every container. Document with photos.
- Install 1-2 BG-Sentinel-2 monitoring traps; place 10-20 ovitraps. Record baseline.
- Treat all containerized water with Bti Dunks/Bits.
- Broadcast AquaBac 200G across gulch zones (~38 lb total).
- Install In2Care stations: 60 minimum (high-use zone perimeter) or 150 (wall-to-wall), 1 per 400 m².
- Place CO2 traps per separate trap research.

**Months 1-3 — Suppression:**
- **Weekly:** Bti retreatment after every heavy rain (>1 in/24h); bromeliad flush; ovitrap counts.
- **Every 4 weeks:** In2Care sachet refills.
- **Every 2-3 weeks:** propane swap on CO2 traps.
- Adjust trap and station placement based on monitoring hot/cold zones.

**Months 3-12 — Maintenance:**
- Monthly cycle. Ovitrap counts every 2 weeks.
- Spot adulticide barrier spray only for events; always evening, never on bloom, never near water.

**Year 2+ — Steady state:**
- If 12-month ovitrap counts show >80% reduction held steady, drop In2Care density to 6/acre in lowest-pressure zones (keep 10/acre at the gulch/bush interface where reinvasion pressure is highest).
- Keep Bti rotation. Keep monitoring — the moment counts climb, you've drifted.

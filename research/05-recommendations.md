## The Mosquito Magnet question — answered honestly

**Your instincts are correct.** The original Mosquito Magnet (American Biophysics Corp, pre-2007) was genuinely excellent. ABC was a research-driven entomology company; the Counterflow CO2 trap technology was breakthrough work and the build quality matched.

**What changed:**

- **2005:** ABC sued contract manufacturer Cowan Plastics over quality defects. Returns spiked, finances collapsed.
- **November 2006:** ABC entered receivership ([Providence Business News](https://pbn.com/mosquito-magnet-creator-american-biophysics-in-receivership22300/)).
- **2009–2010:** Woodstream Corp (Lancaster, PA — same company that makes Victor mouse traps) acquired the Mosquito Magnet IP and brand for ~$6M.
- **Post-2010:** Manufacturing moved offshore, components were cost-engineered down, R&D ceased.
- **2018:** CPSC recall on Quick Clear Cartridge kits — regulator could shatter and shoot fragments at the user. Manufacturer-defect, not user-error.

**Are the negative reviews truthful? Yes — they cluster on real, repeatable failure modes:**

| Failure | Onset | Root cause |
|---|---|---|
| Fast red blink (no ignition) | 12–24 mo | Propane nozzle (0.006" orifice) clogged with sulfur/odorant residue |
| Combustion honeycomb clog | 18–36 mo | Catalyst soot accumulation |
| Igniter failure | 1–3 yr | Silicon nitride hot-surface igniter fatigue |
| On/off switch failure | 1–4 yr | Cheap rocker switch (Woodstream refuses to sell the $0.50 part) |
| Battery (cordless) | ~24 mo | NiMH/Li capacity loss |
| Circuit board (Executive) | 3–4 yr | Moisture intrusion |

The Woodstream-era warranty is **one year**. Many failures occur 12–18 months in. Tech support routes everything to a $180–$300 factory "tune-up" instead of selling a $30 part. BBB has Woodstream documented for a pattern of failure to respond to complaints.

**On Hakalau (windward, 150"+ rainfall): the failure cycle compresses.** Plan annual or twice-annual nozzle teardowns at minimum.

**Should you buy refurbished ABC originals? Tempting, mostly not.** The wear parts that fail (nozzle, igniter, switch, fan motor, battery) are common to both eras and still sourced through Woodstream. A 17-year-old chassis with brand-new wear parts is no better than a new chassis with brand-new wear parts.

**The right call:** skip the Mosquito Magnet ecosystem entirely. There is a better trap.

## Why Biogents BG-Mosquitaire CO2 is the right answer

1. **No combustion = the entire propane failure tree is gone.** No nozzle to clog, no igniter to fatigue, no thermocouple to break, no catalyst honeycomb to soot. CO2 comes from a bottled cylinder (welding-supply grade in Hilo), regulated through a simple solenoid timer.

2. **Peer-reviewed eradication evidence on the same species you have.** Jahir et al. 2022 (*Insects* 13:805, [PMC9503984](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9503984/)) deployed 6 BG-Mosquitaire CO2 + 7.2 BG-GAT per hectare on a 41-hectare Maldives tropical island and achieved **93–98% suppression** of *Aedes albopictus* and *Culex quinquefasciatus*. Knols et al. 2023 (Puerco Island, Philippines, [PMC10531793](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10531793/)) achieved **complete elimination** of the same species in 5 months. There is no equivalent published evidence for Mosquito Magnet, SkeeterVac, Mega-Catch, or DynaTrap.

3. **Right manufacturer.** Biogents is a German entomology firm that manufactures the WHO/CDC reference *Aedes* surveillance trap (BG-Sentinel, cited in 300+ peer-reviewed papers). Two-year warranty (vs. MM's one). 8+ year field reliability in long-term-user reports.

4. **Right price.** $299 per trap with bundle. Mosquito Magnet Executive is $972 for inferior reliability and no eradication evidence.

5. **Right consumables economy.** A 22-lb CO2 cylinder (~$25 refill in Hilo) lasts ~20 days at full duty. With an 8-hr timer covering peak biting hours, opex drops to ~$200/trap-yr. Mosquito Magnet runs ~$300/trap-yr in propane + octenol.

**Scenario B is the recommended buy.** Capex is reasonable for a 15-acre property, operating cost is ~40% less than barrier-spray service ($20K+/yr from VDCI Hawaii or Orkin Kamuela), and it's the only scenario with peer-reviewed precedent for tropical near-elimination.

**Placement principles** (from AMCA + Biogents):

- Trap belt 50 ft inside the property line on the windward (NE) edge — Hawaii trades drive the CO2 plume *across* the protected zone.
- 200 ft spacing on windward edge, 300 ft on leeward.
- Interior traps at 250 ft spacing across the 10 used acres.
- **Place each trap in a sheltered, shaded, low-disturbance spot** — direct sun degrades the lure faster and the trap performs worst in full sun/wind.
- Orient so the CO2 plume drifts toward the area you want to protect.

## The Hakalau context that changes the calculus

**Species you actually have** (per Big Island 2015-16 dengue outbreak entomological survey, Johnston et al. 2020):

- **58% Aedes albopictus** — Asian tiger, aggressive day-biter, sea level to ~4,000 ft. Primary 2015-16 dengue vector.
- **25% Aedes aegypti** — yellow fever / dengue / Zika competent. Common assumption that this is "eradicated from Hawaii" is **wrong** — it has re-established in Big Island pockets.
- **7% Wyeomyia mitchellii** — bromeliad-axil breeder. Guaranteed presence on any Hakalau property with bromeliads or heliconia.
- **5% Aedes vexans** — floodwater nuisance.
- **4% Culex quinquefasciatus** — small share of the human-biting catch but **the most ecologically dangerous mosquito on the property**: the sole avian-malaria vector.
- **1% Aedes japonicus**.

**The avian-malaria / honeycreeper argument** ([Cornell Wildlife Health Center](https://wildlife.cornell.edu/blog/landscape-level-mosquito-suppression-protect-hawaiis-rapidly-vanishing-avifauna)):

Hakalau Forest National Wildlife Refuge sits at 2,500–6,500 ft on the eastern Mauna Kea slope and shelters the densest endemic forest bird assemblage in Hawai'i — 'i'iwi, 'apapane, 'akiapola'au, 'akepa, Hawai'i 'amakihi. Avian malaria, transmitted by *Culex quinquefasciatus*, is the primary driver of honeycreeper extinction. iiwi mortality from a single infectious bite is ~93%.

The malaria zone is climbing. Year-round transmission below ~1,250 m (4,100 ft); historical boundary 1,500 m, currently moving past 1,700 m (5,580 ft) due to warming. **If the parcel is below 4,500 ft, it is in the year-round Culex zone and aggressive suppression directly contributes to honeycreeper recovery** by removing potential infected vectors from the landscape. The property is effectively a buffer zone for the NWR.

**This is real, not greenwashing.** Every wild Culex you kill on the parcel removes a possible bridge between a partially-resistant lowland 'amakihi and a susceptible upslope 'i'iwi or 'akiapola'au.

**Wolbachia IIT status (2025–2026)**:

- Maui (Haleakalā NP): active since Nov 2023, ~500K sterile males/week.
- Kaua'i (Alaka'i Plateau): active.
- **Big Island: planning phase.** April 2024 USFWS statement: 5-year goal to implement at Hakalau Forest NWR or Hawai'i Volcanoes NP. Draft CCP-EA published March 2025.

Private suppression on the parcel is **fully compatible with IIT**. Sterile males die naturally within ~1 week regardless. Killing wild females and breeding sites only improves the math.

## The integrated protocol (don't skip these layers)

The Maldives study didn't get 93–98% from traps alone. It was **traps + larval source management (LSM) + Bti** layered. On a wet 15-acre Hakalau parcel the breeding pressure is much higher than a Maldives island, so you need the full stack.

**Layer 1 — Source reduction (free, weekly walk):**
- Gutters cleared and screened
- Tarps tight or stored
- Bromeliads / heliconia / hapu'u boots: flush with hose weekly OR drop a pinch of Mosquito Bits into each axil
- Plant saucers drained or filled with sand
- Tires drilled or removed
- Boats / kayaks flipped or under tight tarp
- Birdbaths / pet bowls refreshed every 3 days
- A/C condensate pointed to dry surface
- Rain barrels: 1mm stainless mesh on inlet + 1 Mosquito Dunk per month inside
- **Do NOT introduce mosquitofish (Gambusia)** — they're already devastating to native Hawaiian damselflies. Native 'opae and gobies are far better biological control.

**Layer 2 — Bti larviciding (very cheap, very effective, harmless to fish/birds/bees):**
- Mosquito Dunks (~$1/each) in any standing water you can't drain — 30-day persistence
- Mosquito Bits (~$15 / 30 oz) into bromeliad axils, gutter pockets, ornamental plant tanks — 24h kill
- AquaBac 200G (~$170/40 lb bag) broadcast across gulch zones at 2.5 lb/acre — 15 acres × 2.5 lb = ~38 lb/treatment (one bag)
- **Retreat every 7–10 days during heavy rain stretches** (granules wash out faster than label states). Drier weeks: every 14–21 days.

**Layer 3 — In2Care via licensed contractor (the breeding-site amplifier):**
- Mechanism: gravid female enters station to lay eggs, picks up pyriproxyfen + *Beauveria bassiana* fungal spores. She *fails* to lay viable eggs in the station, then visits her natural breeding sites and contaminates each with pyriproxyfen, killing larvae across many sites she'd visit. Fungus kills her in ~10–14 days.
- Field evidence: Manatee County FL (Buckner JAMCA 2017) showed 57–60% reduction in eggs/larvae/adults standalone. Australia (Liu 2023) showed 43% fewer eggs at 6 weeks, 50% adult eclosion reduction.
- **Density requirement: 1 station per 400 m² ≈ 10/acre is non-negotiable.** Below this density, suppression collapses (JME 2024).
- **EPA-restricted to licensed pest-control operators** — cannot buy direct.
- **Recommended for Hakalau:** contract a Big Island pest-control operator to deploy and service ~10 stations in deep gulch / unmanaged-bush zones (the places traps can't reach). ~$400–$700/mo all-in for ~10 stations.

**Layer 4 — Adulticides: minimize, don't broadcast.**

Hakalau's adjacent native ecosystem makes broad-spectrum adulticides ethically and ecologically wrong:
- **Do NOT install permanent mister systems** (Mistking, MistAway). Pyrethroid drift kills bees, butterflies, native Hylaeus pollinators, aquatic invertebrates.
- **Backpack ULV foggers:** pre-event spot use only — 90 min before guests, evening, never within 20 ft of bloom or water.
- **Barrier sprays (Talstar, Bifen IT):** spot only, evening, dry weather, never on flowering plants, never within 30 ft of streams. Note that bifenthrin concentrate is on Hawaii's Restricted Use Pesticides list — most pro formulations need a certified applicator.

**Layer 5 — Personal protection:**
- Permethrin-treated outerwear (Sawyer kit) — clothes only, ~6 weeks / 6 washes
- DEET 20-30% / picaridin 20% / oil of lemon eucalyptus on skin
- Screen porches and lanai (always-on solution)

**Layer 6 — Monitoring:**
- 1–2 BG-Sentinel-2 monitoring traps (gold-standard research trap) for weekly population index
- 10–20 ovitraps (black plastic cup + water + hardboard paddle) for egg counts
- 2-week baseline before starting → measure week-over-week reduction
- Target: 50% reduction by week 8, 80%+ by week 16. If you don't see this curve, density is wrong somewhere.

## Phased rollout

**Weeks 1–2 — Knockdown blitz**
- Walk every acre, document standing water with photos
- Install 1–2 BG-Sentinel-2 monitoring traps + 10–20 ovitraps; record baseline counts
- Treat all containerized water with Mosquito Dunks/Bits
- Broadcast AquaBac 200G across gulches (~38 lb / one bag)
- Order and stage 27 BG-Mosquitaire CO2 + 30 BG-GAT
- Contact licensed Big Island pest-control operator for In2Care quote (Kona Coast Pest Control, Big Island Pest Control, Western Exterminator Hawaii, Orkin Kamuela)

**Weeks 3–6 — Trap deployment**
- Install all 27 BG-Mosquitaire CO2 traps per placement plan (perimeter belt + interior grid)
- Install 30 BG-GAT oviposition traps in shaded ground positions
- Contractor installs ~10 In2Care stations in deep gulch zones
- Confirm CO2 cylinder rental account with Hilo welding/beverage supplier (Big Island Welding Supply or Airgas Hilo)

**Months 1–3 — Suppression**
- Weekly: Bti retreatment after every >1" rain pulse; bromeliad flush; ovitrap counts
- Every 4 weeks: contractor refills In2Care sachets
- Every 2–3 weeks: CO2 cylinder swaps on traps; trap catch-bag empty
- Adjust trap and station placement based on monitoring hot/cold zones

**Months 3–12 — Maintenance**
- Monthly cycle of all the above
- Ovitrap counts every 2 weeks
- Spot adulticide barrier spray only for events; always evening, never on bloom, never near water

**Year 2+ — Steady state**
- If 12-month ovitrap counts show >80% reduction held steady, drop In2Care density to 6/acre in lowest-pressure zones
- Keep Bti rotation, keep monitoring
- The moment counts climb, you've drifted somewhere — re-audit
